As we gear up for the momentous ICC T20 Cricket World Cup scheduled for this year, get ready to have the time of your life! But what is this strange game you ask?

Here are the major rules, explained in terms of Baseball:

1. Bases and Creases:
-Cricket has two bases, marked by creases where batters stand.
-Batters are safe when behind the crease; between creases, they can be run out.
2. Strikes and Stumps:
-Cricket uses physical stumps and bails on bases as a strike zone.
-If the ball dislodges the bails, it's a strike; one strike can lead to being bowled out.
3. Catches:
-Batters can be caught out if a fielder catches a hit ball.
-Unlike baseball, cricket players (except catchers) don't wear gloves, adding challenge to catching.
4. Scoring Runs:
-Cricket is played with bases fully loaded, and runs are scored by running between bases.
-Batters don't return to the dugout after scoring; they stay on bases until put out.
5. Fielding and Runs:
-Cricket's field is fair, allowing batters to hit the ball in any direction.
-Fielding strategies in cricket are more complex due to the larger playing area.
6. Winning the Game:
-Generally, the team with the most runs at the end wins.
-Ties are possible, especially in longer formats, and the team with fewer outs may win in some cases.
